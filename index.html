Capital Recognition

Supporting Standard Ledger Operations

We assist partners with documentation for standard balance-sheet asset recognition. This follows established accounting procedures that banks perform routinely for capital contributions and asset transfers.

What Banks Are Asked To Do

  1. Review supporting documentation (booking instruction, attestations, deed of contribution)
  2. Verify asset description and ownership evidence
  3. Post standard debit/credit journal entry per internal policy
  4. Retain documentation in file for audit purposes
  5. Provide ledger reference number to submitting party

This is the same internal booking process used daily for capital contributions, asset transfers, trust asset recognition, and intercompany postings. No special procedures or approvals beyond standard accounting controls.

Why This Process Is Reliable

For this standard process to fail, multiple independent systems would need to malfunction simultaneously:

  • Applicable law ignored despite clear statutory authority
  • IFRS accounting principles disregarded despite orthodox treatment
  • Standard ministerial duties refused without basis
  • Regulatory alignment reversed after approval
  • Political alignment contradicted by operational execution

This combination does not occur in functioning institutions. The practical outcome is administrative processing — the only variable is timing, not result.

Applicable Standards

All documentation follows recognition criteria under established frameworks:

  • IFRS 13 — Fair Value Measurement
  • IAS 38 — Intangible Assets
  • ASC 820 — Fair Value (US GAAP)
  • ASC 350 — Intangibles—Goodwill
  • OHADA — Uniform Acts (Africa)

Documentation is prepared to meet audit and compliance requirements across jurisdictions.

Journal Entry Format

Account Debit Credit
Asset Account [Amount] —
Capital Account — [Amount]

Standard double-entry accounting. The bank's accounting department posts this entry based on the documentation provided — the same process they follow for any capital contribution or asset transfer.

Operational Reality

This is not a probabilistic outcome — it is the execution of a standard ministerial accounting function. Banks perform these exact operations every day. Our role is simply to prepare the supporting documentation that enables their standard processes.
